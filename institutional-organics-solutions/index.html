The Problem

Institutions need waste solutions that work in the real world.

Institutions face rising hauling costs, sustainability reporting pressure, certification targets, and operational complexity. At the same time, they generate mixed food waste streams that are difficult to manage elegantly.

Distributed food waste generation

Kitchens, cafeterias, and events produce organics across multiple service points.

Post-consumer complexity

Mixed streams require practical separation and handling strategies.

High cleanliness expectations

Odor, appearance, and containment standards are critical in institutional environments.

ESG and diversion pressure

Leadership teams need measurable, report-ready waste outcomes.

Footprint limitations

Many campuses and facilities cannot support large on-site compost operations.

Cross-team coordination needs

Facilities, dining, and sustainability teams need one aligned operating model.

Handling Pre- and Post-Consumer Waste

Different institutional waste streams require different design choices.

The right system design starts with realistic waste assumptions. We help institutions evaluate composition, handling pathways, and circulation constraints before deployment.

1

Stream classification

Map kitchen prep scraps vs. post-consumer material and packaged vs. unpackaged organics.

2

Separation standard design

Define upstream sorting requirements for dining operations and service teams.

3

Site circulation planning

Align service access and movement patterns so the system fits existing operations.

4

Deployment pathway

Recommend a pilot, single-stream line, or multi-pathway configuration based on your goals.
