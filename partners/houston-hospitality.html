The Opportunity

Why The Houston Resort needs on-site biodigestion.

A 1,000-acre destination with theme parks, hotels, dining, and millions of visitors will generate massive volumes of organic waste. Solving this on-site creates cost savings, revenue, and a sustainability story that strengthens your capital raise.

Massive Waste Stream = Massive Opportunity

Theme park food courts, 40+ restaurants, resort kitchens, and landscaping across 1,000 acres will produce thousands of tons of organic waste annually. Hauling this to landfill is the most expensive and least defensible option. On-site BSFL biodigestion converts this waste into premium byproducts at 40–60% lower cost.

Strengthen the Capital Raising Narrative

Investors increasingly scrutinize ESG commitments. A documented, operational waste-to-value system — integrated from design phase — demonstrates circular economy leadership. This differentiates The Houston Resort in LP presentations and institutional due diligence.

Achieve Gold LEED (or Push to Platinum)

The project already targets Gold LEED. Operational waste management is worth up to 8 LEED points — a significant allocation that's often the hardest to earn. BSFL biodigestion with 60% to 80%+ diversion rates can secure the maximum credit and potentially push certification higher when combined with complementary processes.

Micro Farming Integration — Already Planned

The Houston Resort already lists micro farming in its development features. BSFL biodigestion is the ideal engine: larvae feed micro-farm poultry and aquaponics, frass enriches on-site gardens, and the system creates a visible, experiential sustainability feature for guests.

Eco-Lodge & Guest Experience

The eco-lodge concept and Bear Grylls Adventure Zone already signal commitment to nature and sustainability. Visible biodigestion and micro farming become experiential amenities — educational, Instagrammable, and aligned with the brand. Guests see sustainability in action.

Revenue from Day One of Operations

Byproduct sales generate revenue from Year 1 — aligning with the project's distribution timeline. Larvae for animal feed, frass for landscaping and commercial sale, and insect oil for industrial markets. All offtake guaranteed through our established brands.

LEED & ESG Impact

A ready-made solution for the hardest certification points.

Securing Top-Band Certification

Getting the final additional points to tip into the top bands of LEED and similar certifications is notoriously difficult. Operational waste management is one of the largest single-credit opportunities — and one developers often leave on the table because they lack a practical solution.

LEED v4.1 awards up to 8 points for operational waste management based on the percentage of waste diverted from landfill. With BSFL biodigestion achieving 60% to 80%+ diversion, The Houston Resort can secure the maximum allocation — a significant chunk of the points needed for Gold or Platinum.

Beyond LEED, this system supports WELL Building Standard scores and enables pursuit of emerging certifications. The operational data feeds directly into ESG reporting for institutional investors and LP presentations.

For a project of this scale and ambition, incorporating biodigestion during the design phase is far more cost-effective than retrofitting. It should be positioned as core infrastructure, not an afterthought.

Our Proposal

An integrated waste-to-value system for The Houston Resort.

Early Light proposes a phased deployment — from pilot validation through full-scale integration — tailored to the project's construction and operational timeline.

Phase 1: Pilot Validation

Deploy container-based BSFL units during construction. Process construction-phase organic waste. Validate economics and generate sustainability data for LP presentations.

Phase 2: Factory Scale-Up

As the resort opens, scale to automated factory-grade systems. Match capacity to operational waste volumes from restaurants, food courts, hotels, and landscaping.

Phase 3: Micro Farming Integration

Integrate larvae output into on-site micro farming operations. Power poultry, aquaponics, and garden systems that become guest-facing amenities at the eco-lodge.

Phase 4: Education & Community

Deploy Light Forager programming at the Wonderverse Institute and surrounding community. Align with the project's workforce training and educational mission.
